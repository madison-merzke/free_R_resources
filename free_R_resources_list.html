Advance your analytical skills for free with online R resources

Author

Madison Merzke, MAS, BPH

Published

April 13, 2025

How to Use This List

This list of free, online resources for R is organized, first by medium (e-book/website, video, event/community space, etc), and then by subject. It was compiled by data scientists working in the public health and social science spheres for professionals seeking to grow their R skills.

Many thanks to all of the authors, organizers, and contributors in the R world, who have taught us all so much! If you have a resource you’d like to propose we add, please message me on LinkedIn.

Free Online E-Books & Websites

Introductory

R for Data Science - the bible; if you’re new to R and the tidyverse, start here!

The Epidemiologist R Handbook - great introductory resource and reference tailored for applied epidemiology and public health

Important

To save yourself time, get comfortable referencing R package documentation! Most common packages have fantastic, easily accessible online documentation that provide details on how to use them. For example, I use stringr functions with regular expressions all the time and reference that page constantly. I can almost guarantee you that at least half of your R journey will be learning how to answer your own questions using the internet!

Statistical Techniques with R

Tidy Modeling with R - goes deeper into modeling with tidymodels packages

Handbook of Regression Modeling in People Analytics - “a starting point for a practical learning of regression methods,” focused on people analytics

Meta-Analysis Learning Information Center - introduction to using R for meta-analysis

Biostatistics for Biomedical Research - “aimed at exposing biomedical researchers to modern biostatistical methods and statistical graphics, highlighting those methods that make fewer assumptions, including nonparametric statistics and robust statistical measures”

Exploring Complex Survey Data Analysis Using R - “A Tidy Introduction with {srvyr} and {survey}”

Tip

If you’re working with large data sets that take time to read in, trying using vroom() to read them in much faster!

Working with Qualitative Data

Text Mining with R: A Tidy Approach - the basics of NLP with R, a great place to start

Data Visualization

Many of the other resources listed include sections on data visualization. However, the below may provide some additional inspiration:

The R Graph Gallery – Help and inspiration for R charts

Sharing Your Work

Tip

Leverage Quarto and GitHub to create a free, online portfolio of work you can share with your network and use in the job search process!

Happy Git and GitHub for the useR - learn to use Git for version control and to share your code and projects

R Workflow for Reproducible Data Analysis and Reporting - collection of associated best practices

Tidyverse style guide - make your code more readable! You can automate a lot of this with styler, which can now reformat your code in RStudio upon save (within updated version of RStudio)

Quarto - learn about all of the creative, cool outputs you can create with R and Quarto, right in RStudio!

Free Online Videos

Posit::conf(2024) talks - varying topics, but inspiring and super informative

Free Online Events & Community Spaces

Tip

One of the best ways to keep growing in your R skills is to engage with other useRs! There are many conferences and events, both virtually and free or in-person, depending on your preferences and budget! You can also add this into your daily routine by connecting with or following others useRs on LinkedIn, BlueSky, or other social media of your choice! I have learned so much via examples other brilliant useRs have shared.

TidyTuesday - weekly exercises with associated Slack; post your results with #TidyTuesday!

Posit’s Data Science Hangout - weekly call, Thursday at noon EST

Free Online Courses

Many online courses are free or low-cost, such as Coursera’s Statistical Analysis with R for Public Health Specialization. Also, we suggest you consider what free resources you might be able to access through your connections or community. For example, veterans and military spouses can often get a free year-long account on Coursera or Udemy through their programs.
